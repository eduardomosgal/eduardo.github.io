Neurodiversity • STEM Learning

Jardín Mate-mágico

Empowering children with ASD Level 1 to master abstract mathematical concepts through multisensory play.

Real-world interaction: Observing engagement and sensory response in a controlled learning environment.

The Cognitive Bridge

Children with ASD Level 1 tend to be very literal, which makes abstract mathematics (patterns, sets, and operations) a significant challenge. Jardín Mate-mágico acts as a physical bridge, translating these concepts into tangible, interactive exercises.

Learning Focus

Patterns, sets, sequences, and basic operations.

Interaction

Literal physical inputs for abstract mental models.

Inclusive Sensory Engineering

🔊 Auditory & Visual Control

To avoid sensory overload, the main character "Kai" emits sounds at low frequencies (< 1 Hz). The integrated app allows instructions to be repeated as many times as needed, providing a safe reinforcement loop.

💡 Soft Visual Cues

I used soft LED colors for feedback instead of harsh sounds. The toy's overall palette is intentionally vibrant to maintain focus, as neurodiverse children may easily disengage without clear visual hooks.

🌿 Haptic Feedback

The tree leaves feature specific textures designed for easy grip and to help neutralize external stimuli through tactile grounding.

Physical Design & Safety Standards

Safety was a non-negotiable requirement. The physical design follows international ergonomic and safety protocols for children's products.

  • Round Shapes: All edges are rounded to prevent injuries during play.
  • ISO Compliance: 3D printed with PLA+ (Non-toxic) according to international safety standards.
  • Anti-Choke Design: Component sizes exceed standard safety dimensions to eliminate any choking hazards.

Final Reflection

"Designing for neurodiversity goes beyond accessibility — it requires a deeper understanding of how users perceive, process, and respond to their environment. Throughout this project, I learned that small design decisions, such as how feedback is delivered or how interactions are structured, can significantly impact a user’s confidence and engagement. This experience reinforced the importance of designing with intention, where clarity, consistency, and sensitivity are essential to creating meaningful and inclusive experiences."
