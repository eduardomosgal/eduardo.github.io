Jardín Mate-mágico

An interactive learning toy powered by hardware and embedded systems.

Final functional prototype: Integrating physical design with electronic responsiveness.

Context

Jardín Mate-mágico was developed to make learning engaging through physical interaction. This case study focuses on the technical and prototyping aspects, highlighting how hardware and electronics were integrated to create a functional system.

The Challenge

The core challenge was translating abstract mathematical concepts into a literal, physical language for children with ASD Level 1.

We had to engineer a "Sensory Bridge": a system stimulating enough to prevent disengagement, yet strictly controlled in its auditory and visual outputs to avoid the sensory overload often triggered by traditional educational tools.

The Goal & My Role

The objective was to develop a cohesive interactive system where hardware and software components enabled responsive user interactions.

  • Designing and assembling electronic system.
  • Integrating lights, button, speaker, Bluetooth, and microcontroller (Arduino Nano).
  • 3D modeling for internal hardware housing.

Hybrid Interaction Model

Jardín Mate-mágico operates as a Phygital Ecosystem. It’s a collaborative experience where physical play and digital supervision merge to provide real-time reinforcement for the child.

1. Physical Setup

Child assembles the toy components manually.

→

2. Digital Validation

Tutor confirms assembly & selects recipe via App (Bluetooth).

↓

3. Guided Play

Child solves math challenges in the physical space.

←

4. Feedback & Reward

Tutor validates via App; System triggers visual/sound rewards.

Hardware Integration

To support this hybrid flow, I developed a microcontroller-based architecture using Arduino. This ensured stable Bluetooth communication between the mobile app and the toy, as well as low-latency responses for the capacitive sensors and visual feedback system.

This technical foundation was key to maintaining the "Sensory Safety" required for neurodiverse users.

Outcome

A functional interactive prototype capable of responding to user input through a precise combination of physical and digital interactions.

Reflection

Working with hardware introduced challenges like reliability and physical constraints. It reinforced the importance of designing complete systems, not just interfaces. If I were to iterate, I would explore modularity to make the system easier to scale and maintain.
